<p><p>Extruded Polylactic Acid (PLA) fiber is produced from renewable sources like tapioca, sugarcane, and cornstarch. Tapioca-made PLA fibers are derived from cassava roots, offering flexibility and moisture-wicking properties. Sugarcane-made PLA fibers utilize the plant's sucrose, resulting in strong and durable materials, often used in textiles. Cornstarch-made PLA fibers, sourced from corn, provide excellent biodegradability and versatility. Each type contributes to sustainable practices in the textile industry, promoting eco-friendly alternatives to traditional fibers while maintaining high performance.</p></p>
