<p><p>The bioprosthetic heart valve market primarily features two types: porcine aortic tissue valves and bovine pericardial tissue valves. Porcine valves, derived from pig aortic tissue, offer excellent flexibility and hemodynamic performance, making them suitable for patients requiring minimal anticoagulation. Bovine valves, made from cow pericardial tissue, are noted for their durability and biocompatibility, providing longevity in diverse patient demographics. Both types aim to mimic natural heart valve function while minimizing complications associated with mechanical valves.</p></p>
