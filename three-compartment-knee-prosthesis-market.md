<p><p>The three-compartment knee prosthesis market is categorized into cemented and non-cemented bone types. Cemented bone prostheses utilize bone cement to bond the implant securely to the bone, providing immediate stability and enabling early mobilization. Conversely, non-cemented bone prostheses rely on bone ingrowth for stability, allowing the implant to integrate with the surrounding bone over time. Each type has specific advantages based on patient needs, surgical preferences, and activity levels, influencing choices in total knee arthroplasty procedures.</p></p>
